Design of Everyday Things
  • Don Norman
Key Learning

Book emphasizes the importance of user-centered design. He argues that good design makes products intuitive and easy to use by prioritizing human behavior and needs. With a focus on affordances, signifiers, feedback, and error prevention, Norman teaches us how design can reduce friction and ensure smooth, functional user experiences.

Thinking, Fast and Slow
  • Daniel Kahneman
Key Learning

The dual systems of thinking: System 1, which is fast and instinctive, and System 2, which is slow and deliberate. The book delves into cognitive biases and how they influence our decisions, highlighting the importance of understanding human psychology to improve design and decision-making in real-world contexts. It also explores the implications of our mental shortcuts on user experience and judgment.

Hooked
  • Nir Eyal
Key Learning

The Hook Model, a framework for creating products that keep users coming back. The model consists of four phases: Trigger, Action, Variable Reward, and Investment. By tapping into psychological drivers such as desire, unpredictability, and effort, products can form habits that engage users consistently. The book offers a guide to creating products that users rely on and continue to use by design.

The Psychology of Money
  • Morgan Housel
Key Learning

The Psychology of Money explores the emotional and psychological factors that influence our financial decisions. The book emphasizes that wealth is often built through patience, good habits, and a long-term perspective rather than through risky investments or quick financial gains.The book highlights the importance of humility, simplicity, and avoiding the mistakes that derail financial progress.
